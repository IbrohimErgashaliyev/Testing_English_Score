How long

Level: beginner

We use for ** say how long:

We have been waiting for twenty minutes.
They lived in Manchester *** ******* years.

We can also use a noun phrase without for:

Let’s go. We’ve been waiting nearly an hour.
I’ve worked here twenty years.

We use since **** *** present perfect or *** past perfect to say **** something started:

I have worked here ***** December.
They had been watching since seven o’clock in the morning.

We use **** … to/until to say when something ****** and finishes:

**** stayed with us from Monday to Friday.
We will be on holiday from the sixteenth until *** twentieth.

Be careful!

We can use to or until with a noun phrase:

My great-grandmother lived in Liverpool **** 1940 to her death.
** great-grandmother lived in Liverpool from 1940 until *** death.

But we can only use until with a clause: 

My great-grandmother lived in Liverpool from 1940 to she died.
My great-grandmother lived in Liverpool from 1940 until she died.
